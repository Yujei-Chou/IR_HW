Daily, we face a plenty of negative information that can profoundly affect our perception and behavior. as well as between the patient and control groups with information (7.05 +- 2.28, 95% CI [6.05; 8.05], compared to 7.50 +- 2.37, 95% CI [6.46; 8.54]; t(38) = 0.54, p = 0.948, n.s.).The present study examines whether and, if so, to what extent negative information affects social cognition and reasoning in a sample of females with a life-threatening disease such as primary breast cancer. Most important, the outcome points to the unfavorable impact of negative messages on visual social cognition in females with breast cancer.
