The death of a child may result in traumatizing forms of grief, and meaning-making coping with loss seems to be important in prevention of intense psychosocial problems among bereaved parents. It was mainly respondents in the 50-59 age group who mentioned this way of dealing with their crisis (mean 2.72). A total of 28% reported that they always and 29% that they often used nature as a coping method, i.e., 57% used nature always or often as an important resource in coping.
