Introduction and importance
Hydatid disease is an anthropozoonosis caused by the larval stage of the Echinococcus granulosus. In this article we describe a case of duodenal fistulization of hepatic hydatid cyst. The surgical approach was a right subcostal laparotomy, a single hydatid cyst of the segment III of the liver was identified measuring 2 cm with extra parenchymal development.
