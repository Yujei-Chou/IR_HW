The COVID-19 pandemic aftermath has aggravated its traumatic effect to engender a mental health crisis. This limitation has led to debates that staycation programs "have less pronounced effects on wellbeing than do vacations spent outside the person's usual living environment" (, p. 574). Considering also our empirical evidence, it is thus rational to assert that motivated and involved tourists attach great importance to traveling, whereby they actively seek and absorb appealing qualities embedded in a trip in order to cushion the need deficiency.
